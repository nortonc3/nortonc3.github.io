Access to Medical Technology

URL How can we make Medical Technology more Accessible in Low- and Middle-Income Countries?
Site Author Zhendong Song
Abstract In this site, ways to make medical technology more accessible are discussed. The author describes the importance of having access to medical devices, and the issues halting expanding access. One of the big problems is that devices must be delivered to many countries, which adds to their cost due to added expenses throughout the process of delivery. Even when available, staff might not be trained enough to keep the devices working properly. Machines can also fail to work appropriately in certain weather conditions, such as those of underdeveloped countries. In addition to this, some developing countries don't have the proper regulations when it comes to implementing and using advanced equipment. A few solutions are then discussed to provide insight as to how we can work towards fixing this issue in the future. Hopefully one day every country will have access to the same equipment and devices.

URL Access to Medical Devices in Low-Income Countries
Site Author National Academy of Medicine
Abstract This site covers the challenges faced by low-income countries regarding access to medical technology. The authors convey the barriers making successful donations difficult. They discuss the mismatch between equipment that's needed and equipment that's received, sustaining the functions and costs of devices in the long-term, and equipment that's unsustainable or not used. The article also covers approaches to the successful donation of said devices, as well as solutions when it comes to addressing gaps in knowledge. These challenges show that simply donating equipment is not enough. Proper thinking, maintenance, and planning are necessary for long-term success.
