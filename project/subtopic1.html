The Evolution of Technology in Medicine

URL Medical Technologies Past and Present
Site Author Vanessa Rampton, Maria Böhmer, and Anita Winkler
Abstract This site is a journal article exploring the evolution of technology in medicine. It covers three processes within the medical field - recording, examining, and treating. After a brief introduction, the authors begin by discussing record-keeping. They go into depth regarding electronic health records (EHRs), which are computer-based patient records. The history of patient records are mentioned, as well as the many benefits of EHRs. Moving on to the examining phase, the article discusses telemedicine and the virtual consulting room. Finally, do-it-yourself medical devices and practices are referenced. Overall, the article suggests that human medicine has changed drastically, and will continue to do so in the future.

URL Milestones in Medical Technology
Site Author Nicholas Bakalar, Karen Barrow, Jon Huang, and Diantha Parker
Abstract This timeline taken from the New York Times documents milestones within the field of medical technology. It begins with the construction of the first magnifying glass in 1250, and moves forward in history up to the development of an artificial grown from stem cells in 2006. It documents advances in radiology like x-ray, electrocardiogram, and magnetic resonance imaging, surgical techniques such as lobotomy and transplant surgeries (kidney, liver, heart, etc), and helpful machinery such as the catheter and pacemaker. If many of these things weren't invented, medicine and overall life would be very difficult. Anesthesia, the stethoscope, and portable defibrillators are just a few of these inventions that changed medicine and healthcare forever. If we didn't have technology, these may have never existed.
